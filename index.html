Solution Modules

These modules fix the key reasons most fail to grow shoulders. Apply them to improve fueling, recovery, and technique.

The Principle

Muscles don't grow from just going to the gym. They grow from being forced to do more work than they did before. This is non-negotiable.

The System
  • When you can complete all sets of an exercise for 12 reps with good form, you must increase the weight in your next session.
  • If you cannot complete a set for at least 8 reps, the weight is too heavy. Lower it for your next set.
The Reasoning

Forcing your muscles to handle progressively heavier loads is the biological signal that commands them to rebuild bigger and stronger. Without it, you are just exercising, not training for growth.

The Principle

Reps only build muscle if your body is fueled. No hydration, no growth.

The System
  • Add a pinch of salt to your water (500ml–1L).
  • Start sipping halfway through your warm-up, then hit it between sets.
The Reasoning

Salt helps your body absorb and hold water. This intra-workout hydration is the fuel for your pump, keeping your muscles full and your reps strong.

Pro Tip

Want longer pumps and fewer cramps? Swap your salt for Lite Salt. It's a $3 fix for a big upgrade.

The Principle

Most of your results come from a few key lifts done well. You don’t need more time—you need more stimulus per rep.

The System (Time-Crunched Alternative)
  • (SIDE) Lateral Raise (Cable or DB) – 2 sets
  • (SIDE) Raise Partials (Heavy Lateral Swings) – 1 set
  • (FRONT) Overhead Press – 2 sets
  • (REAR) Rear Delt Fly (Machine or DB) – 2 sets
  • Rest only 30-45 seconds between sets.
The Reasoning

This routine prioritizes the highest-impact movements for each part of the shoulder. By minimizing rest and focusing on intensity, you create maximum stimulus in minimum time.

Pro Tip

Effort beats volume. A few hard, focused sets will always build more muscle than ten lazy ones.

The Principle

Shoulders have three parts, and each needs different training. Side delts give you width, so they should be your main focus.

The System
  • Front Delts → Overhead Press
  • Side Delts → Lateral Raises (standard or partials)
  • Rear Delts → Reverse Flys or Face Pulls
The Reasoning

Pressing trains the front. Raises train the sides. Pulls and flys train the rear. By using one direct, high-quality movement for each head, you ensure complete development without wasted effort.

Pro Tip

If you already train chest hard with lots of pressing, your front delts get plenty of work. Prioritize your side and rear delt movements to build the "capped" 3D look.

The Principle

You break down muscle in the gym, but you build it in your sleep. Most guys waste this critical growth window.

The System
  • Sleep 8+ hours a night.
  • Stick to the same bedtime and wake time every day.
  • Make your room completely dark and cool.
  • No caffeine after 2 PM.
The Reasoning

Growth happens at night when recovery hormones peak and your body rebuilds damaged muscle tissue. Consistency and a dark, cool environment maximize sleep quality and hormonal output.

Pro Tip

Sleep is the only legal cheat code you’re not using. Master it, and you'll grow faster than everyone who doesn't.

The Principle

Hitting your protein target isn’t expensive—it’s a matter of buying the right foods. The goal is 200g of protein per day. Non-negotiable.

The System

Build your shopping list around these cheap, high-protein staples:

  • Tuna (in springwater)
  • Protein Greek yogurt
  • Chicken breast
  • Frozen fish fillets (basa, hoki)
  • Whey protein concentrate (1kg bags)
  • Frozen egg whites (bulk bags)
The Reasoning

These foods offer the highest protein-per-dollar ratio. Focusing your budget here allows you to easily hit your daily target without overspending on expensive cuts or branded products.

Pro Tip

Aldi, Coles, and Woolies home brands have the cheapest tuna, yogurt, and frozen fish. For whey, buy from Bulk Nutrients online for the best price per kilogram.

Exercise Vault

Your library of approved movements. Each exercise is selected for maximum efficacy. Refer to this section for technique and execution cues.

🎯 Target: Front Delts

⚙️ Setup
  • Set cables low (bottom position)
  • Stand centered between pulleys
  • Soft bend in elbows
🔥 Key Cues
  • Raise both handles straight up in front — stop just above shoulder level.
  • Control both concentric and eccentric — no swinging.
  • Keep core tight — avoid leaning back.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Overarching lower back.
  • Using momentum to swing the cables.

💡 Quick Tip: Think "drag hands up with your shoulders" — don't pull with arms.

⚙️ Setup
  • Sit upright on a bench with back support.
  • Feet planted, core braced.
  • Dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Press dumbbells straight up — not forward or backward.
  • Lower slowly back to shoulder level.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Flaring elbows too wide.
  • Arching lower back excessively.
  • Bouncing dumbbells off shoulders at bottom.

💡 Quick Tip: Keep your forearms vertical throughout — wrists stacked over elbows to stay locked in.

🎯 Target: Front Delts (primary), Side Delts (secondary), Triceps

⚙️ Setup
  • Bench upright under Smith bar.
  • Bar starts just above shoulder height.
  • Hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  • Bar just in front of face.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Press bar straight up.
  • Lower bar under control to just above upper chest.
  • Keep elbows slightly in front of body.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Letting elbows flare fully out.
  • Arching lower back aggressively.

💡 Quick Tip: Let the bar come down as far as you can for max range.

🎯 Target: Front Delts (press), Side/Rear Delts (pull-apart), Upper Back

⚙️ Setup
  • Stand on resistance band with feet shoulder-width.
  • Grip handles at shoulder height, palms forward.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Press straight up to overhead position.
  • At the top, pull hands apart, pause briefly.
  • Return hands to center, then lower.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Rushing the pull-apart.
  • Leaning back excessively.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “press tall, pull wide” for extra stimulus.

🎯 Target: Front Delts

⚙️ Setup
  • Hold EZ bar with narrow grip, palms facing down.
  • Arms extended fully down.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Raise bar to shoulder height.
  • Keep slight bend in elbows.
  • Control the movement — no swinging.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Overarching lower back.
  • Jerking the bar up with momentum.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “smooth controlled lift with front delts doing all the work”.

🎯 Target: Front Delts (primary), Side Delts (secondary), Triceps

⚙️ Setup
  • Adjust seat so handles start around chin level.
  • Back fully against pad.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Press straight up under control.
  • Stop just short of full lockout.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Overarching lower back.
  • Bouncing off the bottom position.

💡 Quick Tip: Let the machine keep you locked in — your only job is pure shoulder tension.

🎯 Target: Front Delts (primary), Side Delts (secondary), Triceps, Upper Back

⚙️ Setup
  • Grip bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Bar starts at upper chest/collarbone level.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Press bar straight up.
  • Move head slightly back as bar passes face.
  • Stay tight through abs and glutes.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Leaning back excessively.
  • Letting bar drift forward.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “bar moves in a straight line — head moves around the bar.”

🎯 Target: Side Delts

⚙️ Setup
  • Cable height just below hip.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Lead with your elbow.
  • Control the movement up and down.
  • Stop just above shoulder height.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Shrugging traps.
  • Using momentum.

💡 Quick Tip: Behind the back variation for better stretch.

🎯 Target: Side Delts

⚙️ Setup
  • Stand tall, dumbbells at sides.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Lead with elbows, not hands.
  • Raise to just above shoulder height.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Using momentum or bouncing.
  • Bending elbows mid-rep.

💡 Quick Tip: Think "push the dumbbells out sideways, not up."

🎯 Target: Side Delts (primary)

⚙️ Setup
  • Sit upright on flat bench.
  • Dumbbells at sides with slight bend in elbows.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Lead with elbows.
  • Raise out to sides.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Letting traps take over.
  • Turning into a front raise.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “lift out to the sides, not up”.

🎯 Target: Side Delts (primary), Upper Traps (secondary)

⚙️ Setup
  • Use lighter weight than standard.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Raise arms out and up all the way overhead.
  • Control both concentric and eccentric.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Using heavy weight that forces momentum.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “lift out and up in an arc”.

🎯 Target: Side Delts (maximum overload)

⚙️ Setup
  • Use heavy weight.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Short, controlled swings in bottom half of range.
  • Lead with elbows.
  • Keep constant tension.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Turning it into a full body swing.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “controlled power swings” — heavy load, small range.

🎯 Target: Side Delts (pure isolation)

⚙️ Setup
  • Stand sideways to a wall and lean into it.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Lead with elbow.
  • Stay fully locked into the support.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Letting traps take over.
  • Using momentum.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “glide elbow out along a perfect sideways path.”

🎯 Target: Side Delts (primary), Upper Traps

⚙️ Setup
  • Attach rope to low cable pulley.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Pull rope up, lead with elbows high and out.
  • Separate the rope slightly at the top.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Letting elbows drop below wrists.
  • Shrugging too much.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “elbows drive out and up” — the hands follow.

🎯 Target: Rear Delts (primary), Upper Traps

⚙️ Setup
  • Attach rope to mid-level cable.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Pull rope towards your face, elbows high and wide.
  • Squeeze shoulder blades together.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Pulling low towards chest.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “elbows back and wide, hands separate at top.”

🎯 Target: Rear Delts (primary), Upper Back (secondary)

⚙️ Setup
  • Sit facing the pad (chest supported).
🔥 Key Cues
  • Pull arms straight back in wide arc.
  • Lead with elbows.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Letting elbows bend too much.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “open arms wide like spreading wings.”

🎯 Target: Rear Delts (primary), Upper Back (secondary)

⚙️ Setup
  • Hinge at hips, chest over toes, back flat.
🔥 Key Cues
  • Pull dumbbells out wide like opening wings.
  • Lead with elbows.
⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid
  • Turning it into a row.

💡 Quick Tip: Think “wide arc, elbows drive out — not back.”
